Famous (03)

Big Ben

Big Ben, an iconic symbol of London, encompasses the Great Bell housed within the Elizabeth Tower. Completed in 1859, this towering structure stands at 316 feet tall. Its renowned clock, featuring four impressive faces and the Westminster Quarters chime, symbolizes British punctuality and tradition, embodying resilience and history for generations of Londoners.

Lateran Obelisk

The Lateran Obelisk, an ancient Egyptian monument standing at over 100 feet tall, graces the Piazza San Giovanni in Laterano in Rome. Transported to Rome in 357 AD, it symbolizes the city's rich history and architectural grandeur, embodying the cultural exchange between ancient civilizations and the enduring legacy of Rome.

Clepsydra of Ancient Greece

In ancient Greece, the clepsydra, or water clock, was a timekeeping device utilized for various purposes, including regulating speeches in courts and measuring time in athletic competitions. Consisting of a container filled with water that dripped at a controlled rate, it provided a rudimentary yet effective means of tracking time, showcasing early Greek ingenuity in time measurement.

Salisbury Cathedral Clock

The Salisbury Cathedral Clock, an exceptional medieval timepiece dating back to the 14th century, is a marvel of engineering and craftsmanship housed within Salisbury Cathedral in England. This astronomical clock not only displays the time but also tracks the movements of the sun, moon, and stars, symbolizing the intersection of science, art, and spirituality in the Middle Ages.

Prague Astronomical Clock

The Prague Astronomical Clock, a masterpiece of medieval engineering and artistry, adorns the Old Town Hall in Prague, Czech Republic. Dating back to the 15th century, this intricate clock not only tells the time but also displays astronomical information, such as the position of the sun, moon phases, and zodiac signs, captivating visitors with its celestial beauty and historical significance.

H1 Marine Chronometer

The H1 Marine Chronometer, a groundbreaking invention by John Harrison in the 18th century, revolutionized maritime navigation. This precision timekeeping device, essential for determining longitude at sea, maintained accuracy even in harsh conditions. Harrison's ingenuity and craftsmanship paved the way for safer and more accurate voyages, reshaping the course of maritime history.

Hamilton Watch Company's Railway Special

The Hamilton Watch Company's Railway Special timepiece, introduced in the early 20th century, was renowned for its accuracy and reliability, making it a staple for railroad professionals. Built to withstand the rigors of rail travel, this watch adhered to strict standards set by railway companies, ensuring precise timekeeping crucial for safe and efficient train operations.

Grandfather Clock, Thomas Tompion

The Grandfather Clock crafted by Thomas Tompion in the late 17th century stands as a testament to his unparalleled skill and innovation in horology. Known for its majestic stature and intricate mechanisms, Tompion's masterpiece became the epitome of precision timekeeping and luxurious design during the Golden Age of English clockmaking, leaving an enduring legacy in horological history.

John Arnold's Chronometer No. 1

John Arnold's Chronometer No. 1, created in the late 18th century, marked a significant advancement in marine navigation. Renowned for its unparalleled accuracy and robust construction, this pioneering timepiece revolutionized maritime travel by providing sailors with a reliable means of determining longitude. Arnold's Chronometer No. 1 remains a symbol of innovation and excellence in horology.
